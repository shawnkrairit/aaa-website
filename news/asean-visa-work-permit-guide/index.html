ASEAN Visa and Work Permit Guide: What Businesses Need to Know in 2026

March 1, 2026

For businesses deploying staff across Southeast Asia, understanding the visa and work permit landscape is essential. Each ASEAN member state maintains its own immigration framework, and the requirements vary significantly from jurisdiction to jurisdiction.

Key Developments for 2026

Thailand continues to refine its work permit system through the e-Work Permit platform, with the Board of Investment offering streamlined processing for promoted companies. The SMART Visa programme, introduced in 2018, now covers thirteen targeted industries and offers work authorisation without a separate work permit.

Vietnam has expanded its list of eligible positions for foreign workers while maintaining requirements for labour market testing. Indonesia's recent regulatory changes have simplified the process for directors and commissioners of foreign-invested companies. Singapore continues to raise the qualifying salary thresholds for Employment Passes, reflecting its focus on attracting higher-skilled professionals.

Common Requirements Across ASEAN

While each jurisdiction maintains distinct procedures, certain requirements are common across the region: employer sponsorship, proof of qualifications or professional experience, health clearance, and police clearance from the applicant's home country. Processing times range from two weeks in Singapore to several months in certain jurisdictions.
